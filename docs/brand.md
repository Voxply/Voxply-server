# Voxply Brand

## Motto

**"Built for players. Owned by no one."**

## One-liner (README / about pages)

A decentralized platform where players can hang out, talk, and play
together. Voice chat, text messaging, federated alliances of hubs, and
community-built games — all keypair-based identity, no central servers.

---

## Logo brief

### Core concept

The logo should feel like it belongs to the players, not a corporation —
raw, bold, slightly defiant. Think less "SaaS startup" and more "something
a modder would tattoo on their mechanical keyboard."

### Symbol candidates

| Concept | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fragmented hexagon** | Hexagon split into pieces radiating outward — federation without a center, each piece independent but part of a whole. **Preferred.** |
| **Controller + signal** | Controller silhouette merged with signal/voice waves — gaming identity and connectivity in one mark |
| **Key as game icon** | Stylized keyhole or key — keypair identity, "you hold the key", ownership |
| **Crown of nodes** | Crown built from connected nodes — "owned by no one" but sovereign in your own domain |

### Style

- Geometric, flat, works at 16×16 (favicon) and billboard scale
- Dark-background native (UI is dark-first)
- One or two colors max: bold accent (electric blue, violet, or amber) on
  near-black
- No gradients in the mark itself; glow/gradient only for splash screens
- Wordmark: monospace or slightly techy sans-serif, all-lowercase `voxply`
  — approachable but technical

### What to avoid

- Speech bubbles (every chat app has one)
- Microphones (too literal)
- Globe / network clichés
- Anything that reads as VPN or crypto project

### Asset checklist

- [ ] `icon.svg` — master vector, mark only

- [ ] `icon-lockup.svg` — horizontal mark + `voxply` wordmark

> Geometric flat logo mark, fragmented hexagon with pieces radiating
> outward, electric violet on near-black background, no text, single mark,
> clean vector style, works at small sizes.
